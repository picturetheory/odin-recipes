Biang Biang Noodles (Biangbiangmian)

Description

Biang biang noodles, or "belt noodles", are a wide, flat, chewy noodle enjoyed in northern China. They're a little time-consuming to make, but very much worth the effort!

Ingredients

  • 200g flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 125ml water

Instructions

  1. Mix the flour, salt, and water in a bowl until combined.
  2. Knead until the dough ball comes together.
  3. Cover and rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Knead dough for additional 5 minutes.
  5. Separate dough into 6 pieces and roll into 6-inch-long strips.
  6. Coat dough with oil and rest, covered, for an additional 2 hours.
  7. Press a chopstick into the middle of each dough strip (lengthwise) until it leaves a deep impression.
  8. Grab each end of the dough strip and stretch gently, periodically smacking the middle of the dough strip against the countertop.
  9. Tear the dough strip down the middle to form two noodles.
  10. Repeat until all of the dough strips have been stretched into noodles.
  11. Bring a pot of water to a boil and cook the noodles for 1-2 minutes.
  12. Drain the noodles and rinse with cold water to stop cooking and ensure a chewy texture.
  13. Serve topped with scallion oil.
